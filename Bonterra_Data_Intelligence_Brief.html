What AI does
AI is not incidental — it's what makes this viable
Five distinct functions across all three ideas.
Pattern detection
Surfaces trends, correlations, and shifts across millions of records — geographic FPL deterioration, screener score trajectories, migration corridor emergence — that no human analyst could manually identify.
Anomaly & outlier detection
Flags where observed data diverges from expected baselines — a tract where intake FPL is dramatically lower than ACS estimates, a region where behavioral health scores are deteriorating faster than peers.
Predictive modeling
Builds forward-looking models: which geographies are at displacement risk, where service demand will spike, which clients are most likely to disengage before outcomes improve.
Natural language querying
Any user — regardless of technical skill — queries the intelligence layer in plain language: "What does the poverty landscape look like in East Austin zip codes?" No SQL required.
Narrative generation
AI drafts funder reports, grant narratives, and impact summaries directly from structured data — reducing administrative burden on nonprofit staff and deepening platform stickiness.

The full picture
Who are the people being served? What is their economic condition? Where are they, where are they moving, and how are they doing? Bonterra is the only platform positioned to answer all of this from a single data architecture.
FPL + Migration
The FPL layer shows depth of hardship. Migration shows whether it is being solved or relocated. Together: are conditions improving for the people, or just the place?
Migration + Screeners
Displacement creates screener discontinuity — displaced clients may not re-engage, or arrive with a very different intake profile. What does displacement actually do to wellbeing?
FPL + Screeners
Economic hardship and wellbeing are correlated but not identical. A high-FPL area may have stronger social cohesion than a moderate-FPL area with high isolation. The interaction is where the most actionable insight lives.
All three
A funder tracking a place-based portfolio can simultaneously see FPL conditions, whether the population is staying or moving, and how screener scores are trending across grantees — in a single view. No other product offers this.

Before we build
Key open questions
These need answers before any external data product development begins.
Legal & governance — prerequisite
  • What do current customer data agreements permit for aggregation and anonymization?
  • What consent frameworks are customers using at intake — do they cover secondary use of de-identified aggregated data?
  • Who inside Bonterra owns data governance for this layer, and does that policy exist yet?
Data quality
  • Is FPL calculation methodology consistent enough across Apricot customers to support cross-org aggregation?
  • Which screener instruments have sufficient volume and version consistency to support aggregation?
  • What percentage of records have the fields required for each use case?
Architecture
  • A semantic/modeling layer between source systems and the intelligence layer is required before analysis at scale.
  • Geographic crosswalk tables (tract to zip, service area, custom polygon) must be built and maintained.
  • A suppression and disclosure control engine must be designed and validated before any external data products are built.
Research partnership
  • An academic or evaluation partner should be engaged early to validate methodology and provide external credibility.
  • A research advisory board from public health, social work, and policy research could strengthen the product and open research funding pathways.
